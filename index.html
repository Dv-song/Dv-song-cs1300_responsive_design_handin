Chosen Interface: Yale School of Art Landing Page

Part 1: identifying usability problems

Why Yale of School of Art Website?

The website serves as a wiki for the Yale School of Art, allowing both students and staff to make edits. Although the website's open-edit nature poses challenges in maintaining a cohesive design and organization over time, these very characteristics make it a fascinating subject for study and analysis. I am interested in exploring how various individuals with access have collaboratively influenced the website's structure, while utilizing topics and skill sets from the lectures to enhance both its accessibility and design language.

Usability Analysis:

Efficiency

The user flow was not efficient due to:

  • Repeated content on the same page
  • No central navigation system on the page as user scrolls down
  • Repeated sections on Upcoming events for general & School of Art specific events.
  • Layout of the content. Most of the content is displayed on the left side of the page.
  • Interesting since they even have a “sitemap” since it is difficult to navigate the website

Learnability

Users struggle learning and perceiving purpose of each section on the landing page due to:

  • Lack of grouping of relevant elements with coherent designs
  • Too many colors are used without any semantic or systematic meaning. (e.g., Yellow grouping was used to Quicklinks, introduction, community bulletin board, and other information as well)
  • There are multiple links and sections where current students and staff can access to make edits to the wiki. This complicates the organization, making it difficult for users to follow the general structure of the wiki and learn the overall structure of the page.
  • Fonts used and sizing are irregular. Although the website “exists as an ongoing collaborative experiment in digital publishing and information sharing”, such lack of information hierarchy makes it easier to share unorganized information but difficult to learn what's out there.

Memorability

Because the website's purpose is to experiment with digital publishing and information sharing, it is difficult to find and remember information presented due to:

  • No visual distinction of main publishing & information shared
  • Lack of figure ground utilization
  • Multiple repeated sections on upcoming events distracts users from actually accessing the digital publications and information uploaded.
  • Use of primary colors makes it distracting.

Overall Review:

All in all, the Yale School of Art website displays an interesting case study for usability since it is effectively maintained by community members. While it is interesting to note the general structures they have developed over the years, overall use of primary colors, lack of information organization, no information hierarchy, as well as general grouping that enable efficient navigation of the website makes it difficult to achieve the goal the website says in its introductory statement.

Layout Elements:

  • color, grouping, proximity, similarity, closure, continuity, common region, figure ground(Foreground vs. background)

Wave Result:

Wave evaluation pointed out several accessibility issues. The three errors were due to the images used on the landing page lacking alternative texts. Besides lack of alternative texts for images used, it also had issues with color contrasts in some portions. Most interestingly, most of the titles and headings were not actually heading elements not following standard semantic tag practices which makes it difficult to assistive technologies to navigate through the page. In Addition to those founded by Wave, I was concerned how such an evaluation system missed identifying readability issues of some elements(displayed below).

Speed Sketching

In Addition to the 9 speed sketches to experiment with layout options, it was integral to perform critical analysis on the information organization and architecture of the landing page since the main problem was difficult navigation as well as repetition of links and routes. In order to have an effective “ongoing collaborative experiment in digital publishing and information sharing” I believe it needs a more accessible and organized structure to display those experimental publications and work. Final Sketch

Key Design Elements from sketches

  1. Navigation Bar visible and organized for easier navigation
  2. Wanted to maintain the overall structure of the landing page with the left side displaying the content while increasing the readability and accessibility.
  3. Wanted to optimize the information display with custom UI cards for several information types such as Exhibition, Publication, and events.

Incorporating these three key features as well as some of layout optimizations, I could develop a final sketch.
